Q2: How did the Normans influence Irish culture and history?
A2: The Normans had a profound effect on Irish culture and history, initially maintaining a distinct culture and ethnicity but eventually becoming more Irish than the Irish themselves, as evidenced by their surnames being common in the southeast of Ireland.
Q3: In what ways did King David I of Scotland introduce Normans and Norman culture to Scotland?
A3: King David I of Scotland, whose elder brother Alexander I had married Sybilla of Normandy, introduced Normans and Norman culture to Scotland, part of the process some scholars call the "Davidian Revolution," by bringing them to his court and rewarding them with lands.
Q4: What evidence is there that the Normans were in contact with England from an early date?
A4: The Normans were in contact with England from an early date because their original Viking brethren were still ravaging the English coasts, and they occupied most of the important ports opposite England across the English Channel.
