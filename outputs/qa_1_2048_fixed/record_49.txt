Q1: What was the primary form of Norman art in Britain?
A1: Norman art primarily survived as stonework or metalwork, such as capitals and baptismal fonts in Britain.

A2: The Duchy of Normandy originated as a fiefdom established by treaty between King Charles III of West Francia and the Viking ruler Rollo in 911.

Q3: In what way did the Normans influence Irish culture?
A3: The Normans had a profound effect on Irish culture and history, settling mostly in an area known as the Pale and building many castles and settlements, leading to significant cultural and linguistic intermixing over time.
