Q1: What culture did the Normans combine with in Ireland?
A1: Irish.

Q2: In which region of Ireland did the Normans primarily settle?
A2: The Normans settled mostly in an area in the east of Ireland, later known as the Pale.

Q3: How did the Normans influence Irish surnames?
A3: Norman descendants today can be recognized by their surnames, with names like French, (De) Roche, Devereux, D'Arcy, Treacy, and Lacy being particularly common in the southeast of Ireland, especially in the southern part of County Wexford.

Q4: What historical event led to William the Conqueror invading Scotland?
A4: William invaded Scotland in 1072 because Malcolm III of Scotland had come into opposition to William and had disputed Scotland's southern borders.

Q5: Which document mentions the establishment of a Latin monastery by monks from Saint-Evroul in southern Italy?
