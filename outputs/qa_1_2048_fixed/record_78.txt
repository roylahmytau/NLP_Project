Q1: What period did the Anglo-Norman language become distinct from Latin?
A1: During the course of the Hundred Years' War.
Q2: How did the Normans influence the development of English?
A2: The Anglo-Norman language was absorbed into Old English and influenced its evolution into Middle English and then Modern English.
Q3: What is the origin of the term "Normans"?
A3: The term "Normans" comes from the French words Normans/Normanz, meaning "Norseman, Viking," which is derived from Old Low Franconian and Old Norse.
Q4: What architectural style did the Normans introduce to England and Italy?
A4: The Normans introduced a unique Romanesque architectural idiom to England and Italy.
Q5: What specific feature of Norman architecture is mentioned in the document?
A5: Norman architecture is characterized by rounded arches, particularly over windows and doorways, and massive proportions.
