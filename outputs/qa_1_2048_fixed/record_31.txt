Q1: When did the Normans attack Dyrrachium?
A1: In 1185.

Q2: What factors contributed to the destruction of Norman architecture and art during the French Wars of Religion and the French Revolution?
A2: The French Wars of Religion caused wanton destruction due to violence, while the French Revolution led to purposeful destruction of religious objects and rampant pillaging due to societal instability.

Q3: How does Norman art differ between Britain and southern Italy?
A3: Norman art primarily survives as stonework or metalwork in Britain, whereas in southern Italy, it survives plentifully in forms strongly influenced by Greek, Lombard, and Arab styles, including mosaics, sculptured fonts, and capitals.

Q4: What evidence suggests cultural exchange between French Crusaders and Norman churches in southern Italy?
A4: French Crusaders brought French artefacts to southern Italian churches, gifting them to their Norman cousins, resulting in many churches preserving works from France alongside native pieces.

Q5: What documents mention the Norman customary law of Normandy?
A5: Document 2 mentions the Norman customary law of Normandy, specifically noting that it was developed between the 10th and 13th centuries and survives today through the legal systems of Jersey and Guernsey. It also details two customaries in Latin written by judges for use by themselves and their colleagues.
