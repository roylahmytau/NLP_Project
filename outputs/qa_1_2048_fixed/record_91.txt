Q1: Who were the two abbots at Fécamp Abbey?
A1: William of Volpiano and John of Ravenna.
Q2: What significant musical development occurred at Fécamp Abbey under the Italian abbots?
A2: The system of denoting notes by letters was developed and taught.
Q3: Which abbey, under the German abbot Isembard, became a center for musical composition?
A3: La Trinité-du-Mont.
Q4: How did the Norman influence manifest in southern Italy according to Document 3?
A4: Norman artwork survived plentifully in forms strongly influenced by its Greek, Lombard, and Arab forebears, including mosaics and sculptured fonts.
Q5: What notable historical figure fled to Scotland and later married Malcolm III of Scotland?
A5: Edgar Atheling.
