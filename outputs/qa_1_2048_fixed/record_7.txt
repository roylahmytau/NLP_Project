Q1: What religion were the Normans?
A1: Catholic.
Q2: How did Norman art in southern Italy differ from that in Britain?
A2: Norman art in southern Italy survived plentifully in forms strongly influenced by its Greek, Lombard, and Arab forebears, while in Britain, Norman art primarily survived as stonework or metalwork such as capitals and baptismal fonts.
Q3: What evidence suggests the influence of French Crusaders on south Italian churches?
A3: Many south Italian churches preserve works from France alongside their native pieces, indicating the influence of French Crusaders.
Q4: What significant event in English history is attributed to Norman influence?
A4: The Norman conquest of England at the Battle of Hastings in 1066 is attributed to Norman influence.
Q5: How did the Normans contribute to the development of a unique architectural style?
A5: The Normans are noted for their unique Romanesque architectural style.
