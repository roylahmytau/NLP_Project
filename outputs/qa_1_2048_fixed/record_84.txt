Q1: What event marked the beginning of the Norman presence in England?
A1: The Norman Conquest of England in 1066.

Q2: How did the Normans influence the development of legal systems in the Channel Islands?
A2: The customary law of Normandy, developed between the 10th and 13th centuries, survives today through the legal systems of Jersey and Guernsey in the Channel Islands.

Q3: Which individual's influence in Wales is mentioned in the documents, and what role did they play?
A3: Ralph, who was appointed as earl of Hereford by Edward the Confessor, played a role in defending the Marches and warring with the Welsh.

Q4: What was the significance of the Treaty of Saint-Clair-sur-Epte in establishing the Duchy of Normandy?
A4: The treaty offered Rollo and his men French lands in exchange for their protection against further Viking incursions, leading to the establishment of the Duchy of Normandy in the northern part of present-day Upper Normandy down to the river Seine.

Q5: How did the Norman influence spread beyond Europe, according to Document 11?
A5: Norman influence spread to the Crusader states of the Near East, Scotland and Wales in Great Britain, Ireland, and to the coasts of north Africa and the Canary Islands.
