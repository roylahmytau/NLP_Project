Q1: What was the name of the count of Apulia?
A1: Robert Guiscard

Q2: According to Document 4, what was the outcome of the battle for Dyrrachium?
A2: Dyrrachium was betrayed to the Normans by Venetian and Amalfitan merchants who had settled there.

Q3: In which historical period did Jean de Bethencourt and Gadifer de la Salle conduct their expedition?
A3: Between 1402 and 1405.

Q4: What role did Robert Guiscard play in the campaign against the Byzantines?
A4: He led an army of 30,000 men in 300 ships, capturing various cities and attacking Dyrrachium from land and sea.

Q5: Who was the de facto leader of the Crusade during its passage through Asia Minor?
A5: Bohemond of Taranto.
