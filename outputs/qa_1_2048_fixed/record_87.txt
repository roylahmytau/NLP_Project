Q1: What is Norman art's most well known piece?
A1: The Bayeux Tapestry

Q2: Who commissioned the Bayeux Tapestry?
A2: Odo, the Bishop of Bayeux and first Earl of Kent

Q3: Which document mentions the Bayeux Tapestry?
A3: Document 4

Q4: What is the Bayeux Tapestry actually called despite its name?
A4: It is not a tapestry but a work of embroidery

Q5: From which region were the embroiderers of the Bayeux Tapestry likely sourced?
A5: Kent, employing natives from Kent who were learned in the Nordic traditions imported in the previous half century by the Danish Vikings.
