Q1: Who became the King of the Canary Islands?
A1: Bethencourt.
Q2: What was the relationship between the Normans and the Frankish people?
A2: The Normans were culturally and ethnically distinct from the old French aristocracy, most of whom traced their lineage to Franks of the Carolingian dynasty.
Q3: Where did Jean de Bethencourt gather his troops for the Canarian island conquest?
A3: Normandy and Gascony.
Q4: What is the most famous work of Norman art mentioned in the documents?
A4: The Bayeux Tapestry.
Q5: What event led to a large Norman army invading Dyrrachium in 1185?
A5: The further decline of Byzantine state-of-affairs, which paved the road to a third attack.
