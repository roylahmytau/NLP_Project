Q1: What areas did the Normans settle in Ireland?
A1: The Normans settled mostly in an area in the east of Ireland, later known as the Pale, and also built many fine castles and settlements, including Trim Castle and Dublin Castle.

Q2: How did the Normans in Ireland eventually integrate with Irish culture?
A2: With time, the Normans came to be subsumed into Irish culture to the point that it has been said that they became "more Irish than the Irish themselves."

Q3: What evidence can one find in the southeast of Ireland to trace Norman ancestry?
A3: Names such as French, (De) Roche, Devereux, D'Arcy, Treacy, and Lacy are particularly common in the southeast of Ireland, especially in the southern part of County Wexford where the first Norman settlements were established.

Q4: How did the Normans influence architecture in southern Italy?
A4: The Normans incorporated elements of Islamic, Lombard, and Byzantine building techniques into their own, initiating a unique style known as Norman-Arab architecture within the Kingdom of Sicily.
