Q1: What is the origin of the term "Normans"?
A1: The term "Normans" comes from the French words Normans/Normanz, plural of Normant, which means "Norseman, Viking."

Q2: In which region did Roussel de Bailleul attempt to establish an independent state?
A2: Roussel de Bailleul attempted to establish an independent state in Asia Minor.

Q3: How did the Normans contribute to the Armenian state?
A3: Many Normans served in the Armenian state, particularly in Cilicia and the Taurus Mountains, and some led significant military forces within the Armenian army.

Q4: Which historical figure is connected to the introduction of Normans and Norman culture to Scotland?
A4: King David I of Scotland, who married Sybilla of Normandy and spent time at the court of Henry I of England, played a key role in introducing Normans and Norman culture to Scotland.

Q5: What was the outcome of Roussel de Bailleul's attempt to create an independent state?
A5: Roussel de Bailleul's attempt to create an independent state was stopped by the Byzantine general Alexius Komnenos.
