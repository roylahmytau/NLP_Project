Q1: What events led to the destruction of Norman architecture and art during the French Wars of Religion and the French Revolution?
A1: The French Wars of Religion caused the wanton destruction of many Norman edifices due to violence, while the French Revolution, with its assault on religion, caused the purposeful destruction of religious objects and societal instability leading to rampant pillaging.
Q2: How did the relationship between Normans and English influence the succession in England?
A2: The marriage of Emma, sister of Duke Richard II of Normandy, and King Ethelred II of England led to Ethelred fleeing to Normandy in 1013, influencing his sons who stayed in Normandy after Cnut the Great's conquest of England, potentially affecting the succession.
