Q1: Who ruled Cyprus in 1191?
A1: Isaac Komnenos

Q2: What was the significance of Fécamp Abbey and Saint-Evroul Abbey in the 11th century?
A2: Fécamp Abbey and Saint-Evroul Abbey were centers of musical production and education where the system of denoting notes by letters and the staff were developed and taught.

Q3: Which family was descended from an Italo-Norman named Raoul?
A3: The Raoulii were descended from an Italo-Norman named Raoul.

Q4: What was the outcome of Richard the Lion-hearted's fleet after leaving Messina in April 1191?
A4: Richard the Lion-hearted's fleet was dispersed by a storm, and some parts of the fleet, including his sister's and fiancée's boat, were anchored on the south coast of Cyprus.

Q5: Why were several families of Byzantine Greece of Norman mercenary origin during the Comnenian Restoration?
A5: Byzantine emperors were seeking out western European warriors during the Comnenian Restoration.
