Q1: What were the main causes of destruction of Norman architectural and artistic remnants during the French Wars of Religion and the French Revolution?
A1: The French Wars of Religion caused wanton destruction due to violence, while the French Revolution caused purposeful destruction of religious objects and societal instability leading to rampant pillaging.
Q2: How did the Norman influence spread beyond Europe during the medieval period?
A2: Normans spread their influence beyond Europe by joining Turkish forces to destroy Armenian vassal-states, serving in the Armenian state in Cilicia and the Taurus Mountains, and participating in the Crusades under Italo-Norman and Anglo-Norman leaders.
Q3: Which historical figure is associated with the Norman conquest of the Canary Islands, and what was his role?
A3: Jean de Bethencourt, a Norman noble, led the expedition that conquered the Canarian islands of Lanzarote, Fuerteventura, and El Hierro, and later took the title of King of the Canary Islands as vassal to Henry III of Castile.
Q4: What significant event occurred in Limassol involving Richard the Lion-Heart, and who were the key figures involved?
A4: Richard the Lion-Heart conquered the entire island of Cyprus in Limassol, supported by Guy de Lusignan, and appointed two Norman generals, Richard de Camville and Robert de Thornham, as governors of Cyprus.
Q5: What was the nature of the Bayeux Tapestry, and who commissioned it?
A5: The Bayeux Tapestry is a work of embroidery, not a true tapestry, and was commissioned by Odo, the Bishop of Bayeux and first Earl of Kent, using native Kent artisans familiar with Nordic traditions.
