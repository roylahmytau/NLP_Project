Q1: What regions did Norman art primarily survive in Britain?
A1: Norman art primarily survived as stonework or metalwork in Britain, such as capitals and baptismal fonts.

Q2: How did the Normans influence Irish culture and society?
A2: The Normans had a profound effect on Irish culture and history, settling mostly in an area known as the Pale in the east of Ireland and building many fine castles and settlements. They intermixed with the Irish, borrowing from each other's language, culture, and outlook, leading to some Norman descendants becoming "more Irish than the Irish themselves."

Q3: What does the name "Norman" mean, and how did it originate?
A3: The term "Norman" means "Norseman" and originated from Norse raiders and pirates from Denmark, Iceland, and Norway who agreed to swear fealty to King Charles III of West Francia.

Q4: How did the Anglo-Norman language evolve?
A4: The Anglo-Norman language became distinct from Latin and was eventually absorbed into the Anglo-Saxon language of their subjects, evolving into Old English and then influencing the development of Middle English.

Q5: What can we infer about the cultural interactions between French Crusaders and south Italian churches?
A5: We can infer that French Crusaders traveling to the Holy Land brought French artefacts to gift to the churches in southern Italy, resulting in many south Italian churches preserving works from France alongside their native pieces.
