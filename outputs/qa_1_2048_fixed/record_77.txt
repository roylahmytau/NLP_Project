Q1: How did the Normans adapt their system in Normandy and England?
A1: The Normans adopted and worked the growing feudal doctrines of the rest of France into a functional hierarchical system in both Normandy and in England.

Q2: Who were the new Norman rulers culturally and ethnically different from?
A2: The new Norman rulers were culturally and ethnically distinct from the old French aristocracy, who mostly traced their lineage to Franks of the Carolingian dynasty.

Q3: What was the role of Norman knights in Normandy before 1066?
A3: By 1066, Normandy had been exporting fighting horsemen for more than a generation, indicating that Norman knights played a significant military role.

Q4: Where did Norman mercenaries fight besides Italy and the Byzantine Empire?
A4: Norman mercenaries fought against the Pechenegs, the Bulgars, and especially the Seljuk Turks.

Q5: What was Edward the Confessor's approach to bringing Norman influences to England?
A5: Edward the Confessor brought a Norman-educated mind and many Norman counselors and fighters to England, including establishing an English cavalry force, though it never fully took root.
