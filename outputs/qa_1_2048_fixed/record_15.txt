Q1: What part of France were the Normans located?
A1: The north.
Q2: During which significant historical event did the Norman aristocracy often identify themselves as English?
A2: The Hundred Years' War.
Q3: How did the Anglo-Norman language evolve over time?
A3: The Anglo-Norman language was absorbed into the Anglo-Saxon language of their subjects and influenced it, helping in the development of Middle English, which then evolved into Modern English.
Q4: Who conquered England in 1066 and how?
A4: Duke William II of Normandy conquered England by killing King Harold II at the Battle of Hastings.
Q5: What was the relationship between early Norman kings of England and the King of France?
A5: Early Norman kings of England, as Dukes of Normandy, owed homage to the King of France for their land on the continent. They considered England to be their most important holding.
