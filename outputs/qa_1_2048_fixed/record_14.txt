Q1: What event marked Duke William II's conquest of England?
A1: Duke William II of Normandy conquered England killing King Harold II at the Battle of Hastings in 1066.
Q2: How did the early Normans view their holdings in England?
A2: The early Normans considered England to be their most important holding as it brought with it the title of King, an important status symbol.
Q3: What languages blended to form the Norman language?
A3: The Norman language was forged by the adoption of the indigenous langue d'oil branch of Romance by a Norse-speaking ruling class.
Q4: Which group's religion and language were replaced by Catholicism and the Gallo-Romance language in the north of France?
A4: The descendants of Rollo's Vikings and their Frankish wives replaced the Norse religion and Old Norse language with Catholicism and the Gallo-Romance language.
