Q1: In what country is Normandy located?
A1: France
Q2: Who were the Normans and where did they originate from?
A2: The Normans were descended from Norse raiders and pirates from Denmark, Iceland, and Norway who agreed to swear fealty to King Charles III of West Francia.
Q3: What significant historical event affected the architectural and artistic remnants of Norman creativity?
A3: The French Wars of Religion in the 16th century and the French Revolution in the 18th century destroyed much of the architectural and artistic remnants of Norman creativity.
Q4: How did the Normans influence the development of customary law in Normandy?
A4: The customary law of Normandy was developed between the 10th and 13th centuries and survives today through the legal systems of Jersey and Guernsey in the Channel Islands.
Q5: What is the significance of the Bayeux Tapestry in Norman art?
A5: The Bayeux Tapestry is the most famous work of Norman art, not a tapestry but a work of embroidery, commissioned by Odo, the Bishop of Bayeux and first Earl of Kent.
