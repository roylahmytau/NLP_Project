Q1: Who conquered England in 1066?
A1: Duke William II of Normandy.
Q2: What happened to the Anglo-Saxons after the Norman invasion?
A2: The Normans and their descendants replaced the Anglo-Saxons as the ruling class of England.
Q3: Why did early Norman kings of England owe homage to the King of France?
A3: Because they considered England to be their most important holding and they owed homage to the King of France for their land on the continent.
Q4: What title did the early Norman kings of England bring with them?
A4: The title of King.
Q5: Which group of Albanian clans were descended from Normans who served under George Maniaces in the Sicilian expedition of 1038?
A5: The Maniakates.
Q6: Who was Robert Guiscard and how did he gain control over southern Italy?
A6: Robert Guiscard was a Norman adventurer who drove the Byzantines out of southern Italy after obtaining the consent of Pope Gregory VII and acting as his vassal.
Q7: What did Robert Guiscard do after securing the consent of Pope Gregory VII?
A7: He continued his campaign, conquering the Balkan peninsula as a foothold for western feudal lords and the Catholic Church.
Q8: How many men and ships did Robert Guiscard lead in his invasion of Albania in 1081?
A8: He led an army of 30,000 men in 300 ships.
Q9: What was the outcome of the battle between the Normans and the Byzantines at Dyrrachium?
A9: The Normans took Dyrrachium after the Venetian and Amalfitan merchants who had settled there betrayed it to them.
Q10: When did the Normans lose control of Dyrrachium, Valona, and Butrint?
A10: In 1085, after the death of Robert.
Q11: Which two cities did the Normans capture in southwestern Macedonia and Thessaly?
A11: Ioannina and some minor cities.
Q12: What was the significance of the castle named Afranji?
A12: It was named after the Normans, meaning "Franks."
Q13: Who led the force of "Franks" into the upper Euphrates valley in northern Syria?
A13: A Norman named Oursel.
Q14: How many troops were Normans in the Armenian general Philaretus Brachamius' army from 1073 to 1074?
A14: 8,000 out of 20,000 troops.
Q15: What was the name of the castle where the Normans served under the Armenian general Philaretus Brachamius?
A15: Afranji.
Q16: Which two cities were under Norman rule in Italy where Italo-Normans may have traded?
A16: Amalfi and Bari.
Q17: How many Lombards joined Robert Guiscard in his conquest of Calabria and Apulia in Southern Italy?
A17: 2,000–3,000 Lombards.
Q18: What was the outcome of the conflict between the Normans and the Byzantines in the region of Dyrrachium?
A18: The Normans defeated the Byzantines and captured Dyrrachium.
