Q1: What is the most famous work of Norman art?
A1: The Bayeux Tapestry.
Q2: Who commissioned the Bayeux Tapestry?
A2: Odo, the Bishop of Bayeux and first Earl of Kent.
Q3: Which region in France does the term "Norman" originate from?
A3: Normandy.
Q4: When did the distinct cultural and ethnic identity of the Normans emerge?
A4: Initially in the first half of the 10th century.
Q5: What was the initial relationship between the Norse raiders and the native Frankish and Roman-Gaulish populations?
A5: They gradually merged with the native Frankish and Roman-Gaulish populations through generations of assimilation and mixing.
Q6: What was the original purpose of the Norse raiders' incursions into the rivers of France?
A6: Initially destructive.
Q7: In what year was the Duchy of Normandy established?
A7: 911.
Q8: Who was the Viking ruler that established the Duchy of Normandy?
A8: Rollo.
Q9: What treaty was signed between King Charles III of West Francia and Rollo?
A9: The treaty of Saint-Clair-sur-Epte.
Q10: What did the treaty offer Rollo and his men in exchange for protecting against further Viking incursions?
A10: The French lands between the river Epte and the Atlantic coast.
Q11: What was the approximate geographical extent of the original Duchy of Normandy?
A11: The northern part of present-day Upper Normandy down to the river Seine.
Q12: How did the term "Norman" originate?
A12: From "Norseman".
